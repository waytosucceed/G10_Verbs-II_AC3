Key Takeaways


  1. What is a non-finite verb?
    A verb that doesn't show tense and doesn't change with the subject.

  2. What is a gerund?
    A verb form ending in "-ing" that acts as a noun in sentences.

  3. How does a gerund function in sentences?
    We can use a gerund as the subject, object, object of a preposition, or subject complement.

  4. What is a participle?
    A verb form ending in "-ing" or "-ed" that acts as an adjective.

  5. How do we differentiate a gerund from a participle?
    A Gerund is used as a noun, while a participle works as an adjective.

  6. What is an infinitive?
    A verb form, usually "to" plus base verb, functioning as a noun/adjective/adverb.

  7. How does an infinitive function in sentences?
    It functions as a noun, adjective, or adverb explaining purpose/reason.

  8. What is a bare infinitive?
    An infinitive without "to" after verbs like "make," "let," "see," etc.

  9. What is an object complement?
    A word/phrase completing the meaning of the direct object after certain verbs.
